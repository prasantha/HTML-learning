THE AINCIENT KINDOM OF POLONNARUWA



VATADAGEYA

Vatagage built by King Parakramabahu the great and it was later embellished by king Nissankamalla. As with many other Vatadage, It had a stupa in the center. The sandakada (Moonstone) and the two muragals (Guardstone) of this Vatadage are considered to be the best examples of such architectural features belonging to the Polonnarywa period.


SEATED IMAGE (SAMADI STATUE)

The seated image (Samadi Statue) is more than 15 feet tall, and depicts the the dyana mudra. The seat was carved in the shape of a lotus flower, It's base decorated with Makara Image. The image shows signs of mahayana influense.


RANKOTH VEHERA

Rankoth vehera also known as "Rathnamali"and a stone inscription mention that King Nissankamalla was built it. At the four central points are large Vahalkadasa or front entrences enclosures built of brick. The original height of Rankoth vehera may have been almost 180 feet and renovation work carried out by latr rulers. The stupa is located in the center of a large square terrace (Salapathala Maluwa), with sand paths (Weli Maluwa) leading to them.


THIWANKA IMAGE HOUSE

This image house had been built by King Parakramabahu(1153-1186) as part of a Jethawanarama comlex. Since the large image of budda has been curved in 3 places, this has been given the name "Thivanka" which literally means three curves. The inside walls of the buildings has een decorated with frescos which belong to 12 th century.
